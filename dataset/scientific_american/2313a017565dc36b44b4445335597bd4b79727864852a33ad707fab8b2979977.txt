Read Our Latest Issue How a naturalist's observations in the wilds of British Columbia inspired a scientist to discover hidden symbioses--overturning 150 years of accepted scientific wisdom Trevor Goward lets me lead, so we travel through the mixed forest at my pace. This is a nod to his rangy 6'5'' figure and the rapid strides he makes across barely discernible deer and bear paths on his land adjacent to Wells Gray Provincial Park in British Columbia. But mostly he is making space for my observations, my innate way of experiencing the landscape. What engages me? How do I see? I brake in front of a small, white-green growth on the trunk of an aspen. "Look there!" he says excitedly, inviting me to peer into the ragbag lichen through a magnifying lens. Suddenly I am in another world, looking down whorls studded with black dots into little caverns sprinkled with superfine dust. This is a preview.
