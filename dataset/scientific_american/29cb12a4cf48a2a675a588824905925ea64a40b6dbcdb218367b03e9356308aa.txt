Read Our Latest Issue A new report shows how Congress can restore trust in essential federal research For much of my time in public service, there were some things government officials did just because they were the right things to do--which included respecting the research done by government scientists. That respect has faded over recent presidencies; "Sharpie-gate" may have been its death knell. Our ability to keep the public safe and move the country forward economically rests, in large part, on federal research. But that work is being endangered by manipulation for political ends, and the ramifications are vast and should concern all Americans.
