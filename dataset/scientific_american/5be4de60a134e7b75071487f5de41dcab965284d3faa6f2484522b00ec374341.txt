Read Our Latest Issue The great hope for a quick and sweeping transition to renewable energy is wishful thinking That is what well-known advocate Amory Lovins envisaged in 1976. He claimed that by the year 2000, 33 percent of America's energy would come from many small, decentralized renewable sources. Decades later, in July 2008, environmentalist Al Gore claimed that completely repowering the country's electricity supply in a single decade would be "achievable, affordable and transformative."
