Read Our Latest Issue Excavations of stone tools left behind by nonhuman primates are illuminating the origins of technological innovation The tide is rising fast, but the monkeys don't seem to mind. They bicker and loll on the rocks and mangroves farther up the shore, munching quietly on an oyster or enjoying a gentle grooming. The younger ones make a game of jumping from a tree branch into the warm, clear sea below. Like everyone along this coastal stretch of rural Thailand, they live in tune with the daily tidal rhythms.
