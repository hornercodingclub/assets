Scientists now have pinpointed how the imagination can make false memories. Volunteers lying in a magnetic resonance imaging scanner were shown a list of words and told to picture each item. For half the words, a photograph of the matching object was flashed. Afterward, volunteers listened to a random sequence of words corresponding either to photographs they saw, to objects they were only told to imagine, or to items neither seen nor imagined. When volunteers falsely remembered seeing photographs of objects they had only imagined, brain regions critical to generating images became highly activated.
