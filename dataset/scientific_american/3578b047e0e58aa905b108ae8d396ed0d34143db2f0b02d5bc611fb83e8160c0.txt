Read Our Latest Issue We each have two parents, but their genetic contributions to what makes us us are uneven. New research shows we are an amalgam of influences from mom and dad Your memories of high school biology class may be a bit hazy nowadays, but there are probably a few things you haven't forgotten. Like the fact that you are a composite of your parents--your mother and father each provided you with half your genes, and each parent's contribution was equal. Gregor Mendel, often called the father of modern genetics, came up with this concept in the late 19th century, and it has been the basis for our understanding of genetics ever since.
