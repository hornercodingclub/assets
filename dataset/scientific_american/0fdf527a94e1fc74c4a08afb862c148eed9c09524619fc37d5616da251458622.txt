More than 80 authors--professors and politicians, soldiers and scientists--contributed chapters to . But the book's sprawling 1,300 pages never mention the that had killed between 50 million and 100 million people worldwide only five years earlier. And many history textbooks in subsequent decades just note the 1918-1919 flu pandemic as an aside when discussing World War I, if at all. This is a preview.
