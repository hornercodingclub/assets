Read Our Latest Issue Read Our Latest Issue Hotter weather sparks aggression and revolution As the climate heats up, tempers may follow suit, according to a study published in August 2013 in . Analyzing 60 quantitative studies across fields as disparate as archaeology, criminology, economics, geography, history, political science and psychology, University of California researchers found that throughout history and across the world, higher temperatures, less rainfall and more drought were consistently linked to increased violence.
