Read Our Latest Issue Read Our Latest Issue A study finds girls are more likely to become compulsive texters and suffer academically Texting has become the most popular form of communication among people under 30. One recent study found that students spend less than six minutes, on average, on schoolwork before being distracted by social media and texting. For a small percentage of teens, texting becomes compulsive--they may try to text less and fail or feel anxiety and frustration if they are kept away from texting.
