Read Our Latest Issue How far can science advance brain-machine interface technology? Will we one day pipe the latest blog entry or NASCAR highlights directly into the human brain as if the organ were an outsize flash drive? The cyberpunk science fiction that emerged in the 1980s routinely paraded "neural implants" for hooking a computing device directly to the brain: "I had hundreds of megabytes stashed in my head," proclaimed the protagonist of "Johnny Mnemonic," a William Gibson story that later became a wholly forgettable movie starring Keanu Reeves.
