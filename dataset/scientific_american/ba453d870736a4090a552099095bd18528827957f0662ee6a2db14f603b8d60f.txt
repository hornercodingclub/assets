Although Earth is known as the "Blue Planet," very little of that which makes it is of much use to us. Fully 97 percent of the world's water is too salty for us to use to slake our thirst, grow our food or wash our clothes. The wise management of the small amount of available freshwater will pose one of the biggest challenges to mankind during the coming decades.
