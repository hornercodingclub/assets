Read Our Latest Issue Is posting raw results online, for all to see, a great tool or a great risk? The first generation of World Wide Web capabilities rapidly transformed retailing and information search. More recent attributes such as blogging, tagging and social networking, dubbed Web 2.0, have just as quickly expanded people's ability not just to consume online information but to publish it, edit it and collaborate about it--forcing such old-line institutions as journalism, marketing and even politicking to adopt whole new ways of thinking and operating.
