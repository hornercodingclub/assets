Read Our Latest Issue "Ultraprocessed" foods seem to trigger neural signals that make us want more and more calories, unlike other foods in the Western diet Nutrition researcher Kevin Hall strives to project a Zen-like state of equanimity. In his often contentious field, he says he is more bemused than frustrated by the tendency of other scientists to "cling to pet theories despite overwhelming evidence that they are mistaken." Some of these experts, he tells me with a sly smile, "have a fascinating ability to rationalize away studies that don't support their views." This is a preview.
