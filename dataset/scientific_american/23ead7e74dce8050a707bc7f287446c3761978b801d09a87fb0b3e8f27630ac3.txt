Read Our Latest Issue Bariatric procedures are revealing new insights into the dialogue between bowel and brain For Teresa, the first plate of scrambled eggs was a transcendent experience. The Stanford University Medical Center nurse coordinator, 41 years old at the time, had completely lost her appetite in the days after her surgery. She consumed only liquids and only at her surgeon's request. Yet when her interest in eating returned, it was as though something about her relationship with food had fundamentally changed.
