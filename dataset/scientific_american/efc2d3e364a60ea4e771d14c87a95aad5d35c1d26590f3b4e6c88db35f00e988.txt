Read Our Latest Issue Novel materials, 3-D printers and a new way of thinking about design could yield objects capable of assembling themselves and changing shape or function on command The road to self-assembling houses and shape-shifting robots could begin with something as simple as plumbing. Today when we want to build infrastructure for moving water around a city, we take rigid pipes with fixed capacities and then bury them. And the system works well enough--until we need to increase the flow of water to an area or until a pipe breaks. Then we have to dig the whole thing up and replace it. This is a preview.
