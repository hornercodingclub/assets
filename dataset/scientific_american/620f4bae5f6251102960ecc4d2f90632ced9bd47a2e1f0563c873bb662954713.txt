Read Our Latest Issue Computers have great trouble deciphering voices that are speaking simultaneously. That may soon change You are at a party, and Alex is telling a boring story. You are much more interested in the gossip that Sam is recounting to Pat, so you tune out Alex and focus on Sam's words. Congratulations: you have just demonstrated the human ability to solve the "cocktail party problem"--to pick out one thread of speech from the babble of two or more people.
