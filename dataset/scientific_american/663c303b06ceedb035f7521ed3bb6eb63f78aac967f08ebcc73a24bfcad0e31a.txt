Read Our Latest Issue To cope with climate change, we need every strategy we've got Not that long ago "adaptation" was considered a dirty word among climate activists. Their view was that if we could retool our lives to accommodate the consequences of climate change--rising seas, longer wildfire seasons, and a long list of other not so natural disasters--industries and governments would use that as an excuse to avoid a more important job: curbing our emissions of the heat-trapping greenhouse gases that cause these problems in the first place.
