Read Our Latest Issue The world's first digital currency cuts out the middleman and keeps users anonymous IMAGINE IF YOU WERE TO WALK INTO A DELI, ORDER A CLUB SANDWICH, throw some dollar bills down and have the cashier say to you, "That's great. All I need now is your name, billing address, telephone number, mother's maiden name, and bank account number." Most customers would balk at these demands, and yet this is precisely how everyone pays for goods and services over the Internet. This is a preview.
