Read Our Latest Issue 10 projects that push the boundaries of the engineered world  The world's largest water-battling structure isn't a dam--it's a network. More than 10,000 miles of levees, dikes and dams combine to protect the Netherlands from the North Sea. The project took half a century to build, but climate-induced sea-level rise means that Dutch engineers will forever be upgrading Delta Works to keep the country dry. This is a preview.
