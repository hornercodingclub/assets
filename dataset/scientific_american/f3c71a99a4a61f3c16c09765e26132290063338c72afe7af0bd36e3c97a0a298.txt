Read Our Latest Issue Ant colonies work without central control. Knowing how they do this might help us understand other systems that have no leader, from brains to the Internet In the 2015 summer blockbuster , the character Hank Pym, a scientist who has invented a suit that can shrink a person down to the size of an insect, remarks that ants can perform amazing feats, but they need a leader to tell them what to do. Pym wears a small device behind his ear that allows him to instruct the ants to act as a phalanx of attackers that helps the ant-sized human hero defeat an evil mastermind. This is a preview.
