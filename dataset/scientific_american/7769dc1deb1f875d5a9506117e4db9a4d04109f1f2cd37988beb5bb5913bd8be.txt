Read Our Latest Issue Our bodies adjust to the cycle of day and night thanks to specialized neurons in our eyes. Ongoing study of these cells could lead to new treatments for winter depression and other conditions In the 1920s Harvard University graduate student Clyde E. Keeler discovered two surprising facts about mice he had bred in his rented attic room. One, all the progeny were completely blind. Two, despite the animals' blindness, their pupils still constricted in response to ambient light, albeit at a slower rate than did the pupils of sighted mice. This is a preview.
