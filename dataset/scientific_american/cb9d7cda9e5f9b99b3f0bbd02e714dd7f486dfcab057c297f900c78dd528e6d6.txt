Read Our Latest Issue Subtle refinements in brain architecture, rather than large-scale alterations, make us smarter than other animals As far as we know, no dog can compose music, no dolphin can speak in rhymes, and no parrot can solve equations with two unknowns. Only humans can perform such intellectual feats, presumably because we are smarter than all other animal species--at least by our own definition of intelligence. This is a preview.
