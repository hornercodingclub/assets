Read Our Latest Issue Professional licensing: Road to social atherosclerosis? Friedman's warning came at a time when only 5 percent of jobs required licenses. Today the proportion has grown past 20 percent. The states, which obtained the right to grant professional licenses from the U.S. Supreme Court in 1889, now cumulatively recognize at least 800 occupations that require them. But only about 50--including medicine, dentistry, law, engineering, accounting, barbering and cosmetology--are registered in all 50 states. Currently up to half of all professionals are in occupations that require a license, but the extent of licensing varies considerably by state. This is a preview.
