Read Our Latest Issue NASA's New Horizons changed everything we thought we knew about this distant planet As the clock neared 9 P.M. on July 14, 2015, I stood with then NASA administrator Charles Bolden and others in our mission control at the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory in Maryland. Within about a minute we were due to receive the first signals from the New Horizons spacecraft, some three billion miles away, after its daring, one-shot flyby of Pluto and its system of five moons.
