Read Our Latest Issue Originally published in January 1942 "Our army's youngest, smallest, toughest baby has many pet names: jeep, peep, blitz buggy, jitterbug, beetlebug, iron pony, leaping Lena, panzerkiller. The names are all affectionate, for the jeep has made good. I was standing in the hot Mississippi sun while Lieutenant Patrick Summerour, of Camp Shelby, explained the jeep. In front of me was a car 11 feet long, 56 inches wide, 40 inches high--half the height of your family auto and three feet shorter. Summerour pointed to a rear towing hook. 'You pull an anti-tank gun here,' he said. 'Civilians often ask why we do not fight tanks with tanks. The answer is twofold. In the first place, a jeep costs $900; a tank $35,000. Second, these tank-destroyers, towing antitank guns, can swarm round old Schicklegruber's tanks and give 'em hell.''' -- , January 1942 More gems from 's first 175 years can be found on our . 6 hours ago  --  Robert Z.
