Read Our Latest Issue The wild pet trade may surpass habitat loss as a factor in the growing silence of the natural world Conservation biologist David S. Wilcove was on a birding trip to the Indonesian island of Sumatra in 2012, when he began to notice that house after house in every village he visited had cages hanging outside, inhabited by the kinds of wild birds he had expected to see in the forest. One in five households in Indonesia keeps birds as pets. That got him thinking, "What is this doing to the birds?" This is a preview.
