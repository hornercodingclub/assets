Read Our Latest Issue Read Our Latest Issue Caffeine boosts blood pressure in the short term, but habitual drinkers may not face the same risk. Researchers followed 155,594 American women over 12 years (from the ongoing Nurses Health Study) and found an inverted-J-shaped relation between high coffee intake and hypertension risk. Colas, however, demonstrated a linear correlation. Caffeinated soft drinks may harbor unidentified risk-boosting compounds, or perhaps the antioxidants in coffee protect the cardiovascular system.
