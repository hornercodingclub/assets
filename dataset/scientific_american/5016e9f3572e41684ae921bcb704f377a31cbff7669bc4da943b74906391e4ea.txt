Read Our Latest Issue Seven clever tactics animals use to create dazzling hues may lead to sophisticated new technologies The changing hues of a peacock's splendid tail feathers have always captivated curious minds. Seventeenth-century English scientist Robert Hooke called them "fantastical," in part because wetting the feathers caused the colors to disappear. Hooke used the recently invented microscope to investigate the feathers and saw that they were covered with tiny ridges, which he figured might produce the brilliant yellows, greens and blues.
