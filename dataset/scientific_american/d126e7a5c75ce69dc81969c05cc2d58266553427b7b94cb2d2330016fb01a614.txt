Read Our Latest Issue A children's clinic raised and supported by Amish and Mennonites proves that high-tech genetics research can be harnessed right now to prevent disease Levi and Emma Kinsinger operate a small greenhouse in southern Pennsylvania. On November 6, 2002, they traveled 450 miles round-trip by taxi, at a rate of a dollar per mile, to bring their eldest son--Mark--to the Clinic for Special Children in Strasburg, Pa. At age four, Mark was frail and socially detached. He lay on the floor in constant, restless motion. His eyes roamed but did not fix, and he was unmoved by sound. From time to time, a guttural noise escaped his throat as he shook violently. The Kinsingers' question, one I've heard countless times in my work as a pediatrician, gave voice to their quiet desperation: This is a preview.
