Read Our Latest Issue Scientists have at last persuaded silicon to emit laser beams. In a few years, computers and other devices will handle light as well as electrons Now several research groups, starting with mine, have coaxed silicon to produce laser light. The advance could have enormous implications for electronic devices that incorporate lasers and optical amplifiers, which currently rely on lasing materials that are far more costly and less common than silicon.
