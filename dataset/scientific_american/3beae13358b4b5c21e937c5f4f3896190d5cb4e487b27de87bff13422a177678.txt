Read Our Latest Issue Jun-Yuan Chen of the Nanjing Institute of Geology and Paleontology and an international team of researchers identified 10 fossils in the Doushantuo Formation in southwest China, which is between 580 and 600 million years old. The remains, which the researchers christened or "small spring animal," measure just 200 micrometers across and are oval in shape ( ). The team identified a number of organs inside the specimens, including mouths, guts, possible sense organs and body cavities called coeloms. The new finds indicate that the "genetic toolkit" required for two-sided development evolved long before the Cambrian, the authors argue. implies that very early life-forms did not have to be large to be complex.
