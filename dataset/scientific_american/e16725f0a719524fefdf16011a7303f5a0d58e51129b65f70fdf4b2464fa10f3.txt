Read Our Latest Issue Did asteroid strikes during the earth's youth spawn the earliest fragments of today's landmasses? Roiling, incandescent magma and boiling gases covered the earth in the wake of its formation 4.6 billion years ago. Regions of this fiery sea eventually cooled enough to crust over, leaving the planet's first hard rocks floating like slag on the white-hot liquid. But they were nothing more than a thin veneer.
