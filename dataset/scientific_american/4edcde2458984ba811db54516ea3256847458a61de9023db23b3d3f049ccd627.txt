Read Our Latest Issue Read Our Latest Issue The of London releases a list of the richest and greenest tycoons Warren Buffett and Bill Gates are first and second on a list of 100 wealthy individuals who have made hefty investments in clean technology or environmental causes. The news organization, which examined dossiers of the world's richest people, says the 100 tycoons on its Green List are together worth $375 billion. According to the , Buffett, who runs the Berkshire Hathaway investment firm, has poured $230 million into BYD, a Hong Kong company that makes batteries for electric cars. He also holds a major stake in MidAmerican Energy, a large wind energy utility. Gates has invested in several renewable fuels companies, including Pacific Ethanol and Sapphire Energy; the latter intends to produce gasoline from algae. Americans hold 35 of the 100 spots.
