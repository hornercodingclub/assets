Read Our Latest Issue Earth monitoring and high-speed Internet are driving demand For decades the number of satellites orbiting Earth rose at a gentle pace, but growth has soared recently. By July 2019 more than 2,200 satellites were aloft. In the 1980s and 1990s the action was in geosynchronous orbit ( ), says Jonathan McDowell, an astrophysicist at the Center for Astrophysics|Harvard & Smithsonian. But now the action is in the lowest Earth orbits ( ), he says, and increasingly dominated by young companies rather than government, military or academic owners. Today the push is from Starlink--constellations of satellites weighing 260 kilograms, being launched by SpaceX to deliver high-speed Internet. This is a preview.
