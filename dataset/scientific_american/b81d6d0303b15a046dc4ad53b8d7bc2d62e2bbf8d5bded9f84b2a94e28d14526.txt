Read Our Latest Issue Electrical stimulation deep within the brain may alleviate devastating mood disorders "I suddenly feel calm." Our patient, a middle-aged woman who suffered from severe depression, uttered these beautiful words in the operating room just a few seconds after one of us (Lozano) applied electrical stimulation to a selected area deep in her brain. The operation, which took place in 2003 at Toronto Western Hospital, relied on only local anesthesia so that the woman could remain conscious and talk to us. This is a preview.
