Read Our Latest Issue Whether or not machines can quickly answer yes-or-no questions could affect everything from national security to the limits of human knowledge On a snowy day in Princeton, N.J., in March 1956, a short, owlish-looking man named Kurt Godel wrote his last letter to a dying friend. Godel addressed John von Neumann formally even though the two had known each other for decades as colleagues at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton. Both men were mathematical geniuses, instrumental in establishing the U.S.'s scientific and military supremacy in the years after World War II.
