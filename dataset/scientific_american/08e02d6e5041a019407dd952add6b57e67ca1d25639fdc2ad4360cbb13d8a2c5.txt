Read Our Latest Issue The gene that causes a devastating neurodegenerative disease may also have been critical in the evolution of our species For 15 years British insurers have agreed not to use information about a prospective policyholder's genes to determine eligibility for certain life insurance policies. The moratorium has one critical exception. An underwriter can take into account when writing some policies whether a person carries the gene for the malady once known as chronic hereditary chorea and now simply Huntington's disease.
