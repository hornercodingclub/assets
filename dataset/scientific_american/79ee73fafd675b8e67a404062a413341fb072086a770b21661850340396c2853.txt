Read Our Latest Issue Read Our Latest Issue A recently discovered waste-removal system is a linchpin in preserving neural health The brain gets rid of some 1.5 kilograms of waste products a year, equivalent to its own weight. One puzzle: Where does its built-in garbage disposal reside? New findings that reveal detritus-carrying passages--what is called the glymphatic system--traffics the brain's discarded leavings. Much of this activity, moreover, occurs during sleep. Manipulating the glymphatic system may be a new way to treat Alzheimer's and other neurodegenerative diseases by removing buildups of toxic proteins.
