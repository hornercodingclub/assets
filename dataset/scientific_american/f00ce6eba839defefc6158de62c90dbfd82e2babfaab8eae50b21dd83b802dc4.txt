Read Our Latest Issue Read Our Latest Issue Books and recommendations from  by Stephen Budiansky Knopf, 2013 ($27.95) During World War II a relatively unheralded group of civilian researchers, led by British ex-naval officer and future Nobel Prize-winning physicist Patrick Blackett, collaborated on finding ways to subdue the Nazi U-boat fleet. Journalist Budiansky details how Blackett and his colleagues' unconventional application of science and mathematics changed military strategy--for example, by applying probability theory to help determine the location of submarines.
