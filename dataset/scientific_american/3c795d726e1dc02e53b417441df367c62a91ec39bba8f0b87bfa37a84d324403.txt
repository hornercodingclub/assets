Read Our Latest Issue Recent fossil, archaeological and genetic discoveries are revising the rise of our species At the dawning of , our ancestors were born into a world we would find utterly surreal. It's not so much that the climate and sea levels or the plants and the animals were different, although of course they were--it's that there were other kinds of humans alive at the same time. For most of ' existence, in fact, multiple human species walked the earth. In Africa, where our species got its start, large-brained and small-brained also roamed. In Asia, there was , a mysterious group dubbed the Denisovans and, later, --a hobbitlike creature, tiny but for its large feet. The stocky, heavy-browed Neandertals, for their part, ruled Europe and western Asia. And there were probably even more forms, as yet undiscovered. This is a preview.
