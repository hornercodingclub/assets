Read Our Latest Issue Snapping together one-atom-thick sheets of material creates substances with completely new properties--and amazing possibilities Generations of minds have been inspired by Legos, the small, snap-together plastic blocks. These blocks have become fantastic cars, elaborate castles and many other whole creations that are greater than the sum of their parts. Today a generation of materials scientists is being inspired by a new type of Legos: building blocks on the atomic scale. This is a preview.
