Read Our Latest Issue The long-running effort to ditch the decaying, 19th-century artifact that defines the kilogram nears its conclusion As he approached the security checkpoint at Washington Dulles International Airport one afternoon last April, Jon Pratt felt on edge. Stuffed in his camera bag were four solid-metal cylinders, the sorts of objects guaranteed to draw the scrutiny of wary Homeland Security TSA staff. Each cylinder weighed exactly one kilogram. One of them--a gleaming platinum-iridium alloy about half the size of a can of tuna--was worth at least $40,000. (The price of platinum currently hovers around $1,000 per troy ounce, a common unit for precious metals.) The other three consisted of finely machined stainless steel. This is a preview.
