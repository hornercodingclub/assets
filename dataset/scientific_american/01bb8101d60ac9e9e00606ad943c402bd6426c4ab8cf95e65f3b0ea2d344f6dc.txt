Read Our Latest Issue A little-known sense that monitors how we feel inside can go awry, potentially distorting body image Nell (not her real name) was shivering, but she did not realize she was cold. Only when a colleague pointed out her goose bumps and blue lips did she think to put on a sweater. Nor does she register feelings such as exhaustion. "Sometimes I don't realize I'm tired until three in the morning," she says. "I just don't get those clues correctly." This is a preview.
