Read Our Latest Issue Scientists are beginning to understand whether changing cloud cover will accelerate global warming or slow it down I hate clouds. Not because they sometimes bring rain but because they are hard. Clouds come in all shapes and sizes: wispy, high cirrus, puffy cumulus, the low, gray stratocumulus layers that blanket gloomy days. This great diversity makes it difficult to predict how clouds will react worldwide as the earth's atmosphere changes. This is a preview.
