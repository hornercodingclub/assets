Read Our Latest Issue Amyloid, the leading target for dementia therapy, faces skepticism after drug failures In March 2019 biotechnology giant Biogen stopped two big trials of its experimental Alzheimer's disease drug aducanumab because it did not appear to improve memory in declining patients. Then, in a surprise reversal several months later, the company and its partner, Japanese drugmaker Eisai, said they would ask the U.S. Food and Drug Administration to approve the treatment. A new analysis, Biogen said, showed that a subset of people on the highest doses in one trial did benefit from the compound, which dissolves clumps of a protein called beta-amyloid within the brain.
