Read Our Latest Issue New technologies are extracting detailed data from our brains that reveal what we know, have seen or have dreamed. Some of the signals could even fly a plane A pilot sits in the cockpit of a Diamond DA42 light aircraft, mentally working through the steps needed to safely land on the runway ahead. Moments later he touches down without having laid a hand on the controls or stepped on the aircraft's pedals. He is no ordinary pilot--in fact, he is not a pilot at all, and he has just landed his aircraft using brain waves. This is a preview.
