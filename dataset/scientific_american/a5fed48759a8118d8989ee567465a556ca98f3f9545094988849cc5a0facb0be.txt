Read Our Latest Issue Orchid pollinators are surprisingly promiscuous about the plants they like Biologists have long believed that orchid bees and orchids rely on each other in equal measure. The shimmering bees pollinate orchids in return for the flowers' donation of perfumes, which male bees use to attract females. And so it was thought that the two organisms co-evolved. But a study led by Santiago Ramirez, an evolutionary biologist at the University of California, Berkeley, that was published in in late 2011 revealed that the bees arose first, thus suggesting that the two are more independent than previously thought.
