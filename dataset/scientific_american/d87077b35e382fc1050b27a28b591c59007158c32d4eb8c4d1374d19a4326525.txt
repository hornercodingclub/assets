Read Our Latest Issue We can tune our mind to notice the bright side of ambiguous events, bolstering our resilience to stress and anxiety When I was a 14-year-old in a suburb of Dublin, we were at the height of "the Troubles." During this period of civil unrest, our school regularly took in girls from Northern Ireland to get them away from the bomb blasts and shootings in Belfast, some two hours' drive across the border. One of these girls was named Sandra, and she had been at our school for a couple of weeks when one day the two of us decided to walk home for lunch.
