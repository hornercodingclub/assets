Read Our Latest Issue The way we pay for science does not encourage the best results With millions of scientific papers published every year and more than $2 trillion invested annually in research and development, scientists make plenty of progress. But could we do better? There is increasing evidence that some of the ways we conduct, evaluate, report and disseminate research are miserably ineffective. A series of papers in 2014 in the , for instance, estimated that 85 percent of investment in biomedical research is wasted. Many other disciplines have similar problems.
