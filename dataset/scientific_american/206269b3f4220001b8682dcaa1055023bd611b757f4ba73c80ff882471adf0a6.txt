Read Our Latest Issue How does the brain form new memories without ever filling up? Scientists turn to the youngest neurons for answers For many years scientists believed that you were born with all the neurons you would ever get. The evidence for this dogma seemed strong: neuroanatomists in the early 20th century had identified immature neurons under the microscope but only in the brains of mammalian embryos and fetuses, never after birth.
