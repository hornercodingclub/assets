Read Our Latest Issue Space-based transmission of quantum cryptographic keys could make the "unhackable" Internet a reality Perfectly secure cryptography requires no technology more exotic than pencil and paper: Choose a random string of letters and numbers to serve as the key for an enciphered message. Write the key down on a piece of paper, use it once and then burn the paper. The trick is to make sure no one intercepts or tampers with the key--and on the Internet, keys are stolen or tampered with all the time. This is a preview.
