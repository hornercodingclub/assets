Read Our Latest Issue By engineering brain cells to switch on or off in response to light, scientists are unlocking the mysteries of the mind and crafting new remedies for brain disorders In a neuroscience laboratory in Boston, a mouse explores a plastic box, poking its nose into this corner and that. The behavior is normal, but the rodent bears a novel prosthesis: a thin glass optical fiber extends from its head and connects to a laser that can generate brief pulses of blue light. The fiber is directed at a small cluster of cells deep in the brain that manufacture the neurotransmitter dopamine. This is a preview.
