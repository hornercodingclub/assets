Read Our Latest Issue A race is on to create the world's heaviest elements--and to explore the periodic table's "island of stability," where these elements exist for more than a moment The heaviest element that humans have ever found is called oganesson. Each atom of the stuff packs a whopping 118 protons into its dense center. In contrast, hydrogen--the most abundant element in the universe, something you can find in your body, Earth's oceans and even the atmosphere of Jupiter--has only one. Scientists announced the discovery of oganesson in 2006, when a Russian-American team used a particle accelerator in Dubna, Russia, to fire millions of trillions of calcium ions at a target of heavy atoms.
