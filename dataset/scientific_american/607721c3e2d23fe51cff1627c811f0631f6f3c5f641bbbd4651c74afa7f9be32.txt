Read Our Latest Issue One species of soil microbe makes unusually wise communal decisions Eshel Ben-Jacob is interested not only in the genomes of the bacteria he studies but also in their personalities. He compares many to Hollywood celebrities. "On the one hand, we admire them, but on the other hand, we think that they are stupid," says Ben-Jacob, a professor of physics at Tel Aviv University in Israel. In December, though, he and his colleagues published a paper in the journal reporting that a species of soil bacteria he discovered in the mid-1990s, , is surprisingly smart by microbial standards.
