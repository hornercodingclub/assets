Read Our Latest Issue Read Our Latest Issue  Core sediments retrieved by three icebreaker and drill ships revise what is known about the Arctic since about 55 million years ago. For a few million years, the North Pole felt downright Floridian thanks to the presence of greenhouse gases released by some unknown geologic process. The warmth as recorded by the core data is 10 degrees Celsius higher than climate models had predicted.
