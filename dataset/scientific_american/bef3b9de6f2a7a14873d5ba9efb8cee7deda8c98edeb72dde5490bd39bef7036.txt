Read Our Latest Issue Harnessing the organ's own healing properties may help prevent heart attacks and lessen the painful effects of severely narrowed coronary arteries The human heart beats more than 100,000 times every day, pumping almost 2,000 gallons of oxygen-rich blood through the aorta to the rest of the body. About 5 percent of that flow finds its way to two major vessels--the coronary arteries--which channel it to a network of progressively smaller blood vessels that feed each fiber of the cardiac muscle.
