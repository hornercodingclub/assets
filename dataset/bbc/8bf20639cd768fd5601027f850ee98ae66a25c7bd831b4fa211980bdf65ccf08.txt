A walker stumbled on the seal, nicknamed Krill, on a path above the Pembrokeshire coast at Abercastle. Rescuers believe it came out of the water via a gully and "travelled some distance" before being found. It is underweight and has wounds on its body. "This was quite an unusual place to find a seal," said Ellie West, an RSPCA animal collection officer. "However she got there, she was very much lost and unsure where she was going." The seal was taken to the Welsh Marine Life Rescue at Milford Haven before being transferred to RSPCA Stapeley Grange Wildlife Centre in Cheshire for rehabilitation.
