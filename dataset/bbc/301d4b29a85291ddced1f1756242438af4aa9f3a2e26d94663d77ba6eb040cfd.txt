The plans for Reading West feature a new glass and brick building which includes a ticket office, public toilets and a small shop. Secure ticket barriers, a new CCTV system and lighting are also expected be constructed at the Oxford Road site in a bid to improve security. Reading Borough Council said the building work could begin in March and would take about a year to complete. Deputy leader Tony Page said the upgrade was "long overdue", according to the . "The station is in a poor state of repair after decades of underinvestment, despite significant usage, with nearly 500,000 passenger journeys a year," he added. "It's as busy as Tilehurst Station, but it remains very much the Cinderella station in the Reading area."
