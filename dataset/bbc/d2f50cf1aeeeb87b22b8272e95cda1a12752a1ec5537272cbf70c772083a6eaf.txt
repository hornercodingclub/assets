"It is also a time of opportunity with the growing conversation on Irish unity and people's demand for a new and better Ireland," she added. Ms Anderson lost her job as MEP on Friday after the UK officially left the EU. She will now join the other two outgoing MEPs - Naomi Long and Diane Dodds in the Stormont Assembly. It is expected she will become Sinn Fein's key voice on Brexit in the chamber.
