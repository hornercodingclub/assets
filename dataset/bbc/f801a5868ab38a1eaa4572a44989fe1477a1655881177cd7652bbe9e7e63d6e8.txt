The female pup was "half the weight she should be" and had an injury to her nose, the animal charity said. She is now receiving around the clock care at its seal facility, where three other pups are being cared for. The animals are "so young" there is "a long road before we can even think of releasing them back to the wild", Steve Byrne, GSPCA manager said. The seal, which weighs 20kg (3st 2lb), has been named Blaze and joins a and two Jersey pups.
