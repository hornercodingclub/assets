Deena Evans and Michael Hipgrave were injured when they responded to a welfare check at Martyn Smith's home in Wolverhampton on 6 July. Mr Smith entered not guilty pleas to two counts of wounding with intent. Appearing at Wolverhampton Crown Court via videolink, the 52-year-old, of Stephens Close, was remanded in custody ahead of a trial scheduled for 4 May. But Judge Michael Chambers QC said the court would "take every step we can to see if the case can be tried earlier" and ordered a pre-trial review for 20 November. Both paramedics were taken to hospital after the attack, with Mr Hipgrave being discharged later that day after surgery for cuts to his back.
