The work, , depicts Prof Hawking together with the words "be curious". It shows the professor's face and also a silhouette of him in his wheelchair. Local artists Kyle Warwick and Tim Shuker-Yates spent four to five hours on their tribute to the former fellow of Gonville and Caius College, Cambridge. Mr Warwick said Prof Hawking visited his primary school in Newnham, Cambridge, when he was aged about 10 to give a motivational talk during assembly. The 25-year-old freelance artist said: "I did it because I'm Cambridge born and bred and very proud of coming from here. "It was less personal, more for what he did for Cambridge. For the people of Cambridge, he was an inspiration. "With all his theories, he definitely put Cambridge on the map more than it was before." The art appeared on the underside of the bridge, which has long been a focus for community art projects in the area.
