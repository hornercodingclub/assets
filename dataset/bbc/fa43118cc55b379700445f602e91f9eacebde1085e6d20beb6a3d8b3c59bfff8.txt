Shay Patel, from Woburn Sands, Milton Keynes, died aged 13 in September, less than two years after his diagnosis. Niki Patel said her "incredibly kind" son would have wanted "to really help children in the future". She wants people to sign a calling for more government-funded research. The family had no warning that Shay was anything other than a healthy 11-year-old when he came into their room at 05:30 GMT in November 2018. "All of a sudden he had just started to shake into a full seizure," Mrs Patel said. Her "football-mad" son had been to training the night before, as well as attending school. Mrs Patel said "by the time he arrived at A&E, he seemed back to his usual self and laughing and joking" so they were "shocked to the core" to discover the diagnosis.
