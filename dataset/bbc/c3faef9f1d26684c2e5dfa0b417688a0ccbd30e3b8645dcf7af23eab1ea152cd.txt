The 1971 coin, which is silver in colour rather than bronze, was found in a donation box used for unwanted foreign currency. Donations made to the box are used to purchase equipment and fund research at the Royal Berkshire Hospital. The 2p, which was auctioned on eBay, was bought by a collector for PS802.03. Last year, a silver 2p coin found in a new packet of coins minted in 1988, which was found by the owner of a petrol station in Poole, sold at Charterhouse auction house in Sherborne, Dorset, for PS1,200. Silver-coloured coins can be mistakenly struck if a cupro-nickel blank is left inside a barrel during the minting process.
